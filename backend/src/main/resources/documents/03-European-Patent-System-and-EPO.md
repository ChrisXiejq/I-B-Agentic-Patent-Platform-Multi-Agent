# European Patent System and EPO

## Overview

This article introduces the European Patent Office (EPO) and the European patent system, and how they support patent commercialisation.

---

#### What is the European Patent Office (EPO)?

The European Patent Office is the executive body of the European Patent Organisation, which grants European patents through a single application procedure. An applicant files one application with the EPO to seek protection in up to 39 European countries. The EPO conducts search and examination; once granted, the patent must be validated in each designated state. The EPO also supports patent information, analytics, and quality standards that underpin commercialisation.

**References:**
- [EPO – European Patent Convention](https://www.epo.org/applying/european.html)
- [EPO – About the EPO](https://www.epo.org/about-us.html)

---

#### How does the European patent application process work?

Applicants file a single European patent application (directly or via PCT) at the EPO. The EPO performs a search and publishes the application, after which the applicant may request substantive examination. If the application meets legal requirements, the EPO grants a European patent. The patent then enters the national/validation phase: the proprietor validates it in each chosen country, which may involve translation and payment of national fees, unless they opt for a Unitary Patent.

#### What patent information and tools does the EPO provide for commercialisation?

The EPO provides Espacenet for patent search, Patent Register for legal status, and Patent Analytics for trends and landscape analysis. It also publishes studies on patent commercialisation, university patenting, and innovation. These resources help identify relevant patents, assess competitors, evaluate technology landscapes, and support licensing or acquisition decisions.

#### How does the EPO support patent quality and commercialisation?

The EPO aims to grant patents that meet high legal and technical standards, which increases legal certainty and attractiveness for licensing and investment. It cooperates with national offices, provides examiner guidelines, and supports harmonisation through the European Patent Convention. High-quality patents are generally easier to commercialise and enforce across Europe.

**References:**
- [EPO – Patent quality](https://www.epo.org/about-us/patent-quality.html)

---

#### What is the relationship between the EPO and the Unitary Patent?

The EPO administers the Unitary Patent system. From 1 June 2023, applicants can request unitary effect for a granted European patent, obtaining protection in all participating EU Member States with a single registration and renewal fee. The EPO maintains the Unitary Patent Register and collects renewal fees. The Unified Patent Court (UPC) handles infringement and validity disputes for European patents, including unitary patents, in participating states.
