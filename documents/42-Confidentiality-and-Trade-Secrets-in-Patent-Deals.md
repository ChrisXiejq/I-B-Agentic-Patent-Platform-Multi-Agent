# Confidentiality and Trade Secrets in Patent Deals

## Overview

This article addresses confidentiality and trade secret considerations in European patent commercialisation.

---

#### Why is confidentiality important in patent licensing?

Licensing involves sharing technical and commercial information. Confidentiality protects: **technical know-how** – beyond the patent; **business terms** – royalties, strategies; **proprietary data** – in due diligence. Breach can harm competitive position. Licence agreements typically include confidentiality clauses. NDAs are common before detailed negotiations. In Horizon Europe, confidentiality may conflict with open science; grant agreements and consortium agreements define balance.

#### What is the relationship between patents and trade secrets?

Patents require disclosure; trade secrets rely on confidentiality. For commercialisation: **patent-only** – technology is fully disclosed in the patent; **patent + know-how** – licence includes confidential technical knowledge; **trade secret only** – no patent, protection via contract and confidentiality. Many licences combine patent rights and know-how. Trade secrets can extend protection beyond patent term but require active confidentiality measures. EU Trade Secrets Directive harmonises protection.

#### What should confidentiality clauses in licence agreements cover?

Key elements: **definition** – what is confidential; **obligations** – use, disclosure restrictions; **exceptions** – public domain, independently developed, legitimately obtained; **term** – duration of obligations (often beyond licence term); **return/destruction** – on termination; **remedies** – injunctive relief, damages. EU GDPR may apply to personal data shared in negotiations. Governing law affects enforceability.

#### How do universities balance confidentiality and open science?

Universities value publication and open science; industry often requires confidentiality. Balance can be achieved through: **delayed publication** – time-limited embargo for patent filing or commercialisation; **restricted disclosure** – confidential annexes, redacted publications; **negotiated terms** – in sponsored research and licence agreements. Horizon Europe has specific rules on dissemination and confidentiality. TTOs negotiate to protect both academic and commercial interests.

#### What are the consequences of confidentiality breach in Europe?

Consequences may include: **contractual** – breach of NDA or licence, damages, termination; **trade secret misappropriation** – under EU Trade Secrets Directive, injunctions, damages, seizure; **criminal** – in some jurisdictions for severe cases. Prevention through clear agreements, access controls, and staff training is important. Legal advice should be sought when breach is suspected.
