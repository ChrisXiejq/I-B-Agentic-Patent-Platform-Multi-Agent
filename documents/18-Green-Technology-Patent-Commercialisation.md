# Green Technology Patent Commercialisation

## Overview

This article discusses patent commercialisation in green technology and cleantech sectors in Europe.

---

#### What role do patents play in green technology commercialisation?

Patents protect innovations in renewable energy, energy efficiency, carbon capture, circular economy, and sustainable materials. They support: licensing to manufacturers and utilities; attracting investment; partnerships with corporates; and technology transfer to developing markets. The EU Green Deal and climate targets drive demand for green technologies; strong IP can help scale deployment and attract capital. Patent landscaping can identify opportunities and FTO in crowded fields.

#### What are the main green technology areas for patent commercialisation in Europe?

Key areas include: **renewable energy** – solar, wind, hydrogen, biofuels; **energy storage** – batteries, fuel cells; **energy efficiency** – buildings, industrial processes; **electric mobility** – vehicles, charging infrastructure; **circular economy** – recycling, materials recovery; **carbon capture and utilisation**. EPO reports track patenting trends in these areas. European companies and research institutions are active filers; licensing and partnerships support diffusion.

#### Are there EU initiatives to support green tech patent commercialisation?

The EU supports green innovation through Horizon Europe (climate, energy, environment topics), the European Innovation Council, and national programmes. The EU Intellectual Property Office (EUIPO) and EPO provide patent information and analytics. The European Patent Office has published studies on clean energy patenting. Green tech projects often involve multiple partners; IP and exploitation planning should be addressed in consortium agreements.

**References:**
- [European Commission – Horizon Europe](https://research-and-innovation.ec.europa.eu/funding/funding-opportunities/funding-programmes-and-open-calls/horizon-europe_en)

#### What licensing models suit green technology?

Models include: **non-exclusive licensing** – broad diffusion of mature technologies; **exclusive territorial or field-of-use** – for capital-intensive deployment; **technology transfer to developing countries** – on favourable terms (e.g. WIPO GREEN, UN initiatives); **cross-licensing** – in complex, standards-based areas (e.g. batteries). Pooling and platform licensing can accelerate adoption. Green tech licensing may also address sustainability and access objectives alongside commercial returns.

#### How can patent data inform green technology commercialisation strategy?

Patent analytics can reveal: technology trends and maturity; key players and portfolios; geographic distribution; collaboration patterns; white space and opportunities. This supports: identifying licensees or partners; assessing competitive landscape; prioritising R&D and IP; FTO analysis. EPO and commercial tools offer green tech–specific analyses. Universities and PROs can use such data to target commercialisation efforts.
