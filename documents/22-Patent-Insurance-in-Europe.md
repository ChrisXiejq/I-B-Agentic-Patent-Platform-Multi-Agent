# Patent Insurance in Europe

## Overview

This article covers patent insurance products and their role in European patent commercialisation.

---

#### What types of patent insurance exist in Europe?

Main types include: **infringement liability insurance** – covers defence costs and damages if the policyholder is sued for infringement; **enforcement insurance** – covers costs of enforcing the policyholder’s patents against infringers; **abstract assurance / validity insurance** – protects against invalidity of a patent. Products vary by insurer; coverage may include legal fees, court costs, damages, and sometimes lost profits. Deductibles, limits, and exclusions apply.

#### How can patent insurance support commercialisation?

Insurance can: **reduce risk** for licensees and investors; **facilitate licensing** by addressing infringement concerns; **enable enforcement** for patent owners with limited litigation budgets; **support financing** by providing lenders with comfort. SMEs and startups may find insurance particularly useful when entering new markets or licensing to larger partners. Some insurers offer products tailored to European and UPC litigation.

#### What factors affect patent insurance availability and cost?

Insurers assess: strength and scope of the patent; nature of the technology and market; past litigation history; policyholder’s risk profile; geographic scope (e.g. UPC territory). Premiums and limits vary. Patents with strong validity and clear scope are easier to insure. High-risk technologies or crowded fields may face higher premiums or exclusions. Deductibles and co-insurance affect effective coverage.

#### How does the UPC affect patent insurance in Europe?

The UPC creates a single forum for patent disputes in participating states, potentially affecting litigation risk and insurance products. Pan-European injunctions increase potential exposure for defendants; centralised validity rulings affect both owners and challengers. Insurers may adjust products and pricing for UPC litigation. Policyholders should ensure coverage extends to UPC proceedings where relevant.

#### Is patent insurance widely used in Europe?

Patent insurance penetration in Europe is lower than in the US. Factors include: limited awareness; cost; underwriting complexity; and historical reliance on self-insurance or indemnities in licence agreements. However, interest is growing, especially among SMEs and in technology sectors. EU and national initiatives to promote IP finance and risk management may encourage uptake. Brokers and insurers with IP expertise can help identify suitable products.
