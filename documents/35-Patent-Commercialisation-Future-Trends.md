# Patent Commercialisation Future Trends

## Overview

This article discusses emerging trends and future directions for patent commercialisation in Europe.

---

#### How is the Unitary Patent and UPC shaping the future of European commercialisation?

The Unitary Patent and UPC, launched in 2023, are already influencing practice. Early adoption has exceeded expectations. Trends include: consolidation of European patents into Unitary form; centralised enforcement strategies; adjusted licensing negotiations for UPC territory; and opt-out decisions for specific portfolios. As the system matures, pan-European commercialisation is expected to become more streamlined and cost-effective.

#### What role will AI and data analytics play in patent commercialisation?

AI and analytics are increasingly used for: **patent search** – semantic search, prior art; **landscaping** – automated classification and visualisation; **valuation** – predictive models; **matchmaking** – connecting owners with licensees; **FTO** – risk assessment. EPO and commercial tools are incorporating AI. Data quality and interpretability remain important. AI may accelerate deal flow but human judgment will remain essential for negotiation and strategy.

**References:**

- [EPO – AI and patenting](https://www.epo.org/news-events/news/2019/20190129.html)

#### How might the EU SEP regulation affect future SEP commercialisation?

The proposed EU SEP regulation would introduce: an EUIPO registry for SEPs; essentiality checks; and FRAND dispute resolution mechanisms. If adopted, it could increase transparency and reduce litigation. Opposition from some SEP holders may affect the final shape. Regardless, SEP licensing will continue to evolve; implementers and holders will need to adapt strategies to the evolving legal and policy environment.

#### What sustainability and green tech trends affect patent commercialisation?

The EU Green Deal and climate targets drive demand for green technologies. Patent commercialisation in cleantech, circular economy, and sustainability will grow. Trends include: **green licensing** – favourable terms for climate technologies; **pooling** – to accelerate adoption; **impact metrics** – linking commercialisation to sustainability outcomes. Patent landscaping and FTO in green tech will support strategic decisions.

**References:**
- [European Commission – Green Deal](https://commission.europa.eu/strategy-and-policy/priorities-2019-2024/european-green-deal_en)
- [WIPO – WIPO GREEN](https://www.wipo.int/ip-outreach/en/ip_and_climate_change/wipo_green.html)

#### How might IP-backed financing evolve in Europe?

IP-backed lending and patent collateral are underdeveloped in Europe compared to the US. Trends that could support growth include: **policy focus** – EU and national initiatives on IP finance; **valuation standards** – better methodologies and data; **lender capability** – specialised products and expertise; **awareness** – outreach to SMEs. Research shows significant untapped potential (e.g. €7–10 billion in additional debt capacity in Sweden and Netherlands); realisation depends on ecosystem development.
