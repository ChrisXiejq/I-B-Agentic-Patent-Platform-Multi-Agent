# Energy Sector Patent Commercialisation

## Overview

This article addresses patent commercialisation in the European energy sector, including renewables, storage, and grid technologies.

---

#### What are the main patent areas in the European energy sector?

Key areas include: **renewable generation** – solar (PV, CSP), wind, hydro, geothermal; **energy storage** – batteries, pumped hydro, hydrogen, thermal storage; **grid and smart grid** – transmission, distribution, demand response; **hydrogen** – production, storage, fuel cells; **carbon capture and storage (CCS)**; **energy efficiency** – buildings, industrial processes. EPO and national offices track patenting trends. European companies and research institutions are active filers.

- [EPO – Patent analytics and studies](https://www.epo.org/searching-for-patents/business/patent-analytics.html)

#### How does the EU Green Deal affect energy patent commercialisation?

The Green Deal and Fit for 55 targets drive investment in clean energy. Demand for relevant technologies increases commercialisation opportunities. EU funding (e.g. Horizon Europe, Innovation Fund) supports R&D and deployment. Patent strategies should align with policy priorities and funding calls. Licensing and partnerships can accelerate technology diffusion. Sustainability-linked licensing may gain traction.

**References:**
- [European Commission – Green Deal](https://commission.europa.eu/strategy-and-policy/priorities-2019-2024/european-green-deal_en)
- [European Commission – Clean energy](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/topics/renewable-energy_en)

#### What licensing models suit energy technology?

Models include: **non-exclusive** – for mature technologies (e.g. solar panels) with broad adoption; **exclusive territorial or field** – for capital-intensive deployment (e.g. grid-scale storage); **technology transfer** – to developing markets on favourable terms; **joint ventures** – for large projects. Energy projects often involve multiple technologies; licensing may be part of EPC or partnership structures. Standards (e.g. for hydrogen, smart grid) may create SEP licensing needs.

#### What role do patents play in battery technology commercialisation?

Battery technology is critical for EV and grid storage. Patenting is dense; licensing and cross-licensing are common. Battery standards may emerge, potentially creating SEPs. European initiatives (e.g. European Battery Alliance, Important Project of Common European Interest) support battery value chains. Patent landscaping and FTO are important for new entrants. Universities and PROs license to industry; corporates use licensing for technology access.

#### How can Horizon Europe support energy patent commercialisation?

Horizon Europe funds energy R&D (e.g. climate, energy, mobility topics). Beneficiaries must exploit results; the Horizon Results Platform supports matchmaking. Joint projects produce jointly owned IP; consortium agreements should address exploitation. The EU IP Helpdesk and Horizon IP Scan assist with IP strategy. Energy projects often involve utilities, manufacturers, and research institutions; clear IP arrangements enable commercialisation.
