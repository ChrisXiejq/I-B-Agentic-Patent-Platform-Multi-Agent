# Patent Landscaping for Commercialisation

## Overview

This article explains how patent landscaping supports commercialisation strategy and decision-making in Europe.

---

#### What is patent landscaping?

Patent landscaping is the analysis of patent data to map the technological and competitive environment in a field. It involves: searching and collecting relevant patents; categorising by technology, applicant, geography, and time; and visualising trends, clusters, and gaps. Landscapes can cover a technology area, industry, or competitor. They support R&D strategy, FTO, licensing, M&A, and policy analysis.

**References:**

- [EPO – Patent searching](https://www.epo.org/searching-for-patents.html)

#### How can patent landscaping support commercialisation decisions?

Landscapes help: **identify opportunities** – white space, emerging technologies, underserved applications; **find licensees or partners** – companies active in the field; **assess competition** – key players, portfolio strength, coverage; **support valuation** – comparables, market size; **inform FTO** – blocking patents, design-around options; **target filing strategy** – where to seek protection. Universities, PROs, and companies use landscapes to prioritise commercialisation efforts.

#### What data sources and tools support patent landscaping in Europe?

The EPO provides Espacenet (search), Patent Register (legal status), and Patent Analytics. Commercial tools add advanced analytics, visualisation, and workflow. Data can be filtered by: technology classification (IPC, CPC); applicant; country; date; legal status. For European focus, EPO and national data are essential. Landscape quality depends on search strategy, classification, and analytical rigour.

#### What are common outputs of a patent landscape study?

Outputs include: **technology maps** – clusters and relationships; **applicant rankings** – by filing count, citations, geography; **timeline charts** – evolution of the field; **geographic heat maps** – where protection is sought; **gap analysis** – underserved areas; **competitor profiles** – portfolio composition and focus. Reports may include recommendations for R&D, IP strategy, and commercialisation. Tailored visualisations support stakeholder communication.

#### How does patent landscaping support university technology transfer?

Universities use landscapes to: prioritise invention disclosures for patenting and commercialisation; identify industry partners and licensees; understand competitive context for valuation and negotiation; support grant applications and impact narratives; inform researchers about prior art and opportunities. TTOs may conduct landscapes in-house or commission external studies. Landscapes can be shared (in part) with potential partners to demonstrate market relevance.
