# Unitary Patent and Unified Patent Court

## Overview

This article explains the Unitary Patent and Unified Patent Court (UPC), and their impact on patent commercialisation in Europe.

---

#### What is the Unitary Patent?

The Unitary Patent provides uniform protection across all participating EU Member States through a single application and a single registration procedure at the EPO. After a European patent is granted, the proprietor may request unitary effect instead of (or in addition to) national validation. A Unitary Patent requires one renewal fee paid in one currency (euro) and covers all participating countries, reducing complexity and cost for commercialisation across the EU.

#### When did the Unitary Patent system start and how is it performing?

The Unitary Patent system went live on 1 June 2023. By February 2025, the EPO had registered over 48,000 unitary patents, and more than 700 cases had been brought before the Unified Patent Court. SME uptake has exceeded initial expectations. The system is expected to improve legal certainty and lower costs for patent commercialisation across participating countries.

#### Which countries participate in the Unitary Patent system?

As of 2024, 18 EU Member States participate: Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, the Netherlands, Portugal, Romania, Slovenia, and Sweden. Romania joined in September 2024. These countries represent about 80% of EU GDP. Spain and Croatia are not part of enhanced cooperation; other EU members may join when they ratify the UPC Agreement.

#### What is the Unified Patent Court (UPC)?

The UPC is a specialised court for European patents (including Unitary Patents) in participating states. It began operating on 1 June 2023 and has exclusive competence for infringement and validity actions in its jurisdiction. A single UPC proceeding can cover multiple countries, replacing parallel litigation in national courts. This reduces cost and inconsistency for patent owners and licensees.

#### How does the Unitary Patent benefit patent commercialisation?

Benefits include: **lower costs** – single renewal fee and fewer translations; **simpler administration** – one registration, one court system; **clearer enforcement** – centralised UPC litigation; **broader market coverage** – up to 25 countries with one step; **easier licensing** – clearer geographic scope. These features make European patents more attractive for licensing, investment, and cross-border commercialisation.
