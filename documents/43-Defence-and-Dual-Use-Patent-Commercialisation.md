# Defence and Dual-Use Patent Commercialisation

## Overview

This article addresses patent commercialisation in defence and dual-use technology in the European context.

---

#### What are dual-use technologies and how do they affect patent commercialisation?

Dual-use technologies have both civilian and military applications. Examples: drones, encryption, sensors, AI, materials. Commercialisation may be subject to: **export controls** – EU and national regulations on transfer of sensitive technology; **security classifications** – restrictions on disclosure and access; **licence conditions** – limitations on use, territory, end-users. Patent filing itself is generally not restricted, but licensing and transfer may require authorisation. Due diligence should identify dual-use character and applicable controls.

#### What export control considerations apply to patent licensing in Europe?

EU export control regulations (e.g. Dual-Use Regulation) may require licences for: transfer of technology (including by electronic means) to certain destinations or end-users; provision of technical assistance. Licensing to entities in sanctioned countries or for military end-use may be prohibited or restricted. Compliance is the responsibility of the exporter. Legal advice is essential for defence or dual-use technology licensing.

**References:**
- [European Commission – Dual-use export control](https://policy.trade.ec.europa.eu/eu-trade-relationships/countries-and-regions/export-controls/dual-use-controls_en)
- [EU IP Helpdesk](https://intellectual-property-helpdesk.ec.europa.eu/)

#### How do defence-specific IP considerations affect commercialisation?

Defence contracts often include: **IP clauses** – government rights, background/foreground; **security** – classified information handling; **restrictions** – on sublicensing, export. Defence agencies may retain march-in or licence rights. Commercialisation to civilian markets may require separation from defence programmes. Defence innovation units and TTOs in defence research organisations manage these complexities.

**References:**
- [European Defence Agency](https://eda.europa.eu/)
- [EU IP Helpdesk](https://intellectual-property-helpdesk.ec.europa.eu/)

---

#### What EU initiatives support defence and dual-use innovation?

The European Defence Fund (EDF) supports collaborative defence R&D. Horizon Europe includes certain dual-use topics. The EU aims to strengthen defence technological and industrial base. IP and exploitation rules in defence funding may differ from civilian programmes. Beneficiaries must comply with grant conditions and export control obligations. Commercialisation pathways may include spin-off to civilian markets where permitted.

**References:**
- [European Commission – European Defence Fund](https://defence-industry-space.ec.europa.eu/eu-defence-industry/european-defence-fund-edf_en)
- [European Commission – Horizon Europe](https://research-and-innovation.ec.europa.eu/)

---

#### How can patent landscaping support defence technology commercialisation?

Landscaping can identify: technology trends in defence-relevant areas; key players (industry, research); white space and opportunities; FTO; potential partners. Care must be taken with sensitive technologies; some analysis may need to be conducted under appropriate security. Landscaping supports R&D strategy, partnership identification, and commercialisation planning for both defence and civilian applications where permitted.
