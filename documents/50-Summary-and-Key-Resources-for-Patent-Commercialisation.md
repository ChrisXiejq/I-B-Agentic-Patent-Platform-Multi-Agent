# Summary and Key Resources for Patent Commercialisation

## Overview

This article provides a summary of key themes and a consolidated list of resources for patent commercialisation in Europe.

---

#### What are the key themes in European patent commercialisation?

Key themes include: **Unitary Patent and UPC** – simplified pan-European protection and centralised enforcement since 2023; **university and PRO commercialisation** – licensing as main channel, TTOs as enablers; **Horizon Europe** – exploitation obligations, IP management, Horizon IP Scan; **valuation and economics** – income, market, cost methods; **SEPs and patent pools** – FRAND licensing, EU regulation; **ecosystem** – ASTP, TTO Circle, EU IP Helpdesk; **sector diversity** – MedTech, automotive, energy, AI, agri-tech, and more.

#### Where can patent owners find support for commercialisation in Europe?

Support sources: **EU IP Helpdesk** – information, helpline, Horizon IP Scan; **EPO** – patent information, analytics, studies; **ASTP** – TTO network, training; **European TTO Circle** – RTO collaboration; **Horizon Results Platform** – matchmaking; **national offices** – patent information, support; **brokers and consultants** – for specific transactions. SMEs and Horizon beneficiaries have dedicated support. TTOs are the primary interface for universities and PROs.

**References:**
- [EU IP Helpdesk](https://intellectual-property-helpdesk.ec.europa.eu/)
- [EPO](https://www.epo.org/)
- [ASTP](https://astp4kt.eu/)
- [European TTO Circle](https://knowledge4policy.ec.europa.eu/technology-transfer/european-technology-transfer-offices-tto-circle_en)

#### What legal and regulatory frameworks govern patent commercialisation in Europe?

Frameworks include: **EPC** – European Patent Convention; **Unitary Patent and UPC** – for participating states; **EU competition law** – TTBER for licensing; **Horizon Europe** – grant agreement, exploitation; **national laws** – contract, IP, tax; **EU regulations** – export control, GDPR, sector-specific. Commercialisation must comply with applicable rules. Legal and tax advice is recommended for complex transactions.

#### What are the main commercialisation pathways for patents?

Pathways include: **licensing** – exclusive, non-exclusive, field or territory limited; **assignment** – outright sale; **spin-off** – new company with patent rights; **in-house use** – in own products or processes; **partnership** – joint development, co-exploitation. Choice depends on resources, strategy, and market. Licensing is dominant for universities; companies may combine pathways. Horizon Europe encourages exploitation through these routes.

#### What trends will shape patent commercialisation in Europe in the coming years?

Trends include: **Unitary Patent uptake** – growing adoption and UPC case law; **SEP regulation** – potential EU framework; **AI and analytics** – enhanced search, landscaping, valuation; **sustainability** – green tech licensing and impact; **IP finance** – expansion of IP-backed lending; **ecosystem strengthening** – policy, training, networks. Stakeholders should monitor developments and adapt strategies. The European patent commercialisation environment is evolving to support innovation and growth.
