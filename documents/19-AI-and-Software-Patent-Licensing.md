# AI and Software Patent Licensing

## Overview

This article addresses patent considerations for artificial intelligence and software commercialisation in Europe.

---

#### Are software and AI innovations patentable in Europe?

Yes, but subject to exclusions. The European Patent Convention excludes "programs for computers" as such from patentability, but technical inventions that involve software—e.g. controlling a process, improving computer operation, or solving a technical problem—can be patented. AI and machine learning inventions are increasingly granted when they have a technical character (e.g. image processing, optimisation of technical systems). The EPO has published examination guidelines for AI and machine learning.

**References:**
- [EPO – Patentability of AI and machine learning](https://www.epo.org/news-events/news/2019/20190129.html)
- [EPO – Guidelines for examination](https://www.epo.org/law-practice/legal-texts/html/guidelines/e/index.htm)

#### What licensing models are common for AI and software patents in Europe?

Models include: **perpetual or term licences** – one-off or recurring fees; **usage-based** – tied to volume, users, or API calls; **field-of-use** – e.g. healthcare vs retail; **white-label or OEM** – embedding in third-party products; **cross-licensing** – in standards-based or platform ecosystems. AI patents often bundle with data, models, or software; hybrid agreements may combine IP licence with data or service terms. SaaS and cloud deployment affect licence structures.

#### How does the Unitary Patent affect AI and software patent commercialisation?

The Unitary Patent provides uniform protection across participating EU states, simplifying pan-European licensing for AI and software. A single patent can cover multiple markets; the UPC offers centralised enforcement. For fast-moving AI sectors, the ability to enforce or defend in one proceeding can reduce cost and uncertainty. SMEs and startups can benefit from streamlined European coverage when expanding across the EU.

#### What are the challenges in valuing AI and software patents?

Challenges include: rapid technology evolution; difficulty isolating patent contribution from data and algorithms; lack of comparable licences; uncertainty over validity and enforceability; short product cycles. Valuation may combine income, market, and cost approaches. Expert input on technical and market factors is often needed. For AI, the interplay of patents, trade secrets (e.g. training data, models), and copyright adds complexity.

#### What EU regulations affect AI commercialisation and IP?

The EU AI Act (2024) establishes a risk-based framework for AI systems. High-risk AI may face stricter requirements (documentation, conformity, monitoring). IP strategy should account for compliance and transparency obligations. Data used for training may be subject to GDPR and data governance rules. Patent and data strategies should be aligned for AI commercialisation in Europe.
