# Automotive Sector Patent Commercialisation

## Overview

This article addresses patent commercialisation in the European automotive industry.

---

#### What are the main technology areas for automotive patent commercialisation in Europe?

Key areas include: **electric vehicles (EV)** – batteries, motors, power electronics; **autonomous driving** – sensors, AI, software; **connectivity** – V2X, infotainment; **powertrain** – hybrid, hydrogen, fuel cells; **materials** – lightweighting, composites; **manufacturing** – automation, additive manufacturing. European OEMs and suppliers are major patent filers. Licensing and cross-licensing are common; standards (e.g. for connectivity) involve SEPs and FRAND.

- [EPO – Patent analytics](https://www.epo.org/searching-for-patents/business/patent-analytics.html)

#### How does the transition to electric vehicles affect patent licensing?

The EV transition creates demand for battery, charging, and power electronics technology. Patent licensing and partnerships (e.g. battery cell supply, charging networks) are increasing. Some automakers license-in technology; others license-out. Battery patents may be subject to standardisation and pool formation. Cross-licensing between automakers and tech companies is growing as software and connectivity become central.

**References:**

#### What role do SEPs play in automotive patent commercialisation?

Connectivity standards (e.g. 4G/5G, C-V2X) and potentially other automotive standards involve SEPs. Implementers (OEMs, tier-1 suppliers) need licences from SEP holders. FRAND licensing and patent pools (e.g. Avanci for automotive) have emerged. Disputes over FRAND rates and licensing level (component vs end-device) continue. The EU SEP regulation proposal aims to increase transparency and support FRAND determination.

#### How do automotive OEMs and suppliers approach patent licensing?

Approaches vary: **defensive** – building portfolios for cross-licensing; **revenue** – licensing non-core technology; **partnership** – joint development with shared IP; **acquisition** – buying companies for technology. Tier-1 suppliers often license to multiple OEMs. Licensing negotiations may involve package deals, field restrictions, and territorial carve-outs. The Unitary Patent simplifies pan-European licensing for automotive technologies.

**References:**
- [EPO – Unitary Patent](https://www.epo.org/en/applying/european/unitary/unitary-patent)

---

#### What EU regulations affect automotive patent commercialisation?

Regulations on emissions, safety, type approval, and data affect product development and technology choices. The EU Battery Regulation addresses sustainability and due diligence. Digital and AI regulations (e.g. AI Act) apply to automated driving. Patent strategy should align with regulatory requirements. Standards supporting regulation (e.g. safety, emissions) may create SEP licensing needs.
