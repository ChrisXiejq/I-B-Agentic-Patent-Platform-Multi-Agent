# Dispute Resolution in Patent Licensing

## Overview

This article covers dispute resolution options for patent licensing and commercialisation in Europe.

---

#### What types of disputes arise in patent licensing?

Disputes may concern: **contractual** – breach, interpretation, royalty calculation; **validity** – patent challenge by licensee or third party; **infringement** – alleged infringement by licensee or third party; **ownership** – who owns or has rights. Contractual disputes are typically resolved under the licence’s dispute resolution clause. Validity and infringement may be addressed in courts (including UPC) or, for some aspects, in arbitration.

#### What dispute resolution mechanisms are available?

Options include: **negotiation** – direct discussion; **mediation** – neutral facilitator; **arbitration** – binding decision by arbitral tribunal; **litigation** – national courts or UPC. Many licences provide a stepped process: negotiate, then mediate, then arbitrate or litigate. Arbitration offers confidentiality and flexibility; litigation (especially UPC) offers precedent and public resolution. Choice depends on preferences for speed, cost, confidentiality, and enforceability.

**References:**
- [WIPO Arbitration and Mediation Center](https://www.wipo.int/amc/en/)

---

#### When is arbitration suitable for patent licensing disputes?

Arbitration suits: **contractual disputes** – royalties, scope, breach; **confidentiality** – parties prefer private resolution; **cross-border** – enforceability under New York Convention; **expertise** – party-appointed arbitrators with IP experience. Limitations: **validity** – in many jurisdictions, only courts can invalidate patents; arbitration may address contractual consequences of validity. UPC has exclusive competence for infringement and validity of UPC patents in its jurisdiction; arbitration cannot replace that for those matters.

**References:**
- [WIPO – Arbitration](https://www.wipo.int/amc/en/arbitration/)
- [EPO – UPC](https://www.epo.org/en/applying/european/unitary/upc)

---

#### How does the UPC affect dispute resolution for European patents?

For European and Unitary Patents in participating states, the UPC has exclusive competence for infringement and validity. Parties cannot opt for national courts (unless they opted out before litigation). UPC offers: single proceeding for multiple countries; specialised patent judges; procedural efficiency. Opt-out remains available for some patents to stay in national courts. Licence disputes about contract terms are not UPC competence; they follow the licence’s dispute resolution clause.

**References:**
- [EPO – Unified Patent Court](https://www.epo.org/en/applying/european/unitary/upc)
- [Unified Patent Court – Jurisdiction](https://www.unified-patent-court.org/)

---

#### What should licence agreements include for dispute resolution?

Agreements should specify: **governing law** – which jurisdiction’s law applies; **dispute resolution** – negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or litigation; **venue** – where proceedings occur; **language** – for proceedings and documents. For arbitration: rules (e.g. ICC, LCIA, WIPO), seat, number of arbitrators. Clarity reduces uncertainty and supports enforceability. Consider also: escalation steps, notice periods, and cost allocation.
