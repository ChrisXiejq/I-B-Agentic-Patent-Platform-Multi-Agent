# Patent Litigation and Commercialisation

## Overview

This article explores the relationship between patent litigation and commercialisation strategies in Europe.

---

#### How does the Unified Patent Court (UPC) change patent litigation in Europe?

The UPC, operational since June 2023, provides a single, specialised court for European and Unitary Patents in participating states. One action can cover multiple countries, reducing the cost and complexity of parallel national proceedings. The UPC can grant pan-European injunctions and damages. Patent owners can also "opt out" of UPC jurisdiction for traditional national litigation. The UPC affects licensing negotiations, enforcement strategies, and risk assessment for commercialisation.

#### When might patent litigation support commercialisation?

Litigation can: **enforce rights** – stop infringement and recover damages; **strengthen negotiating position** – encourage settlement and licensing; **deter competitors** – signal willingness to enforce; **clarify scope** – court rulings define claim interpretation. Some patent owners use strategic litigation to monetise portfolios (e.g. licensing under threat of suit). However, litigation is costly and risky; commercialisation through voluntary licensing is often preferable when possible.

#### What are the risks of patent litigation for commercialisation?

Risks include: **invalidity** – patent may be revoked, weakening the portfolio; **cost** – litigation can be expensive, especially in multiple jurisdictions; **injunction** – if the patent holder loses, they may face counterclaims; **reputation** – aggressive enforcement can affect business relationships; **uncertainty** – prolonged disputes can delay deals. Due diligence and FTO analysis before product launch can reduce the likelihood of disputes.

#### How do SEP and FRAND disputes affect commercialisation?

SEP holders must offer FRAND licences; implementers must negotiate in good faith. Disputes often concern what constitutes FRAND terms and whether parties have negotiated fairly. European courts have developed case law on FRAND obligations, injunctions, and damages. The proposed EU SEP regulation would add registry, essentiality checks, and FRAND dispute resolution. These developments shape SEP licensing strategies and commercialisation.

#### Can arbitration be used for patent disputes in Europe?

Yes. Arbitration is commonly used for licensing disputes, offering confidentiality, flexibility in procedure and remedies, and enforceability under the New York Convention. Parties can choose arbitrators with patent expertise. Some arbitral institutions have specific IP rules. Arbitration does not replace the UPC for validity (which remains with courts); it is well suited for contractual disputes, royalty determinations, and FRAND disagreements.
