# Patent Commercialisation Metrics

## Overview

This article discusses metrics and KPIs for measuring and improving patent commercialisation performance in Europe.

---

#### What metrics do universities and PROs use for patent commercialisation?

Common metrics include: **input** – invention disclosures, patent applications filed, patents granted; **output** – licences executed, licence income, spin-offs created; **efficiency** – time from disclosure to licence, conversion rates; **impact** – products on market, jobs created, economic impact. ASTP and similar bodies support benchmarking. The EPO’s patent commercialisation scoreboard for European universities provides comparative data on commercialisation patterns.

#### What metrics support corporate patent portfolio commercialisation?

Corporate metrics may include: **portfolio** – size, coverage, quality indicators; **licensing** – revenue, deal count, pipeline; **enforcement** – cases, outcomes, damages; **alignment** – patents per product, FTO coverage; **cost** – renewal spend, cost per patent. Metrics should align with business strategy (e.g. defensive vs revenue-generating). Regular reviews support pruning and strategic refocusing.

**References:**

#### How can metrics support Horizon Europe exploitation reporting?

Horizon Europe beneficiaries must report on exploitation. Metrics may include: exploitation started (yes/no); type (direct, licence, transfer, etc.); partners; revenue or impact. The Horizon Results Platform tracks publications and matchmaking. Good metrics help demonstrate impact to the Commission and support future funding. They also inform internal commercialisation strategy.

#### What patent quality metrics support commercialisation?

Quality indicators include: **legal** – grant rate, opposition outcomes, claim scope; **technical** – citations received, technological relevance; **commercial** – licensing or enforcement success. High-quality patents are easier to license and enforce. EPO and commercial tools provide citation and classification data. Quality assessment can inform filing, maintenance, and commercialisation prioritisation.

**References:**
- [EPO – Patent quality](https://www.epo.org/about-us/patent-quality.html)
- [EPO – How do you measure patent value?](https://www.epo.org/en/service-support/faq/searching-patents/patent-management-and-valuation/how-do-you-measure-patent)

#### How do European benchmarks compare to other regions?

European university commercialisation rates and income have historically lagged behind the US (e.g. AUTM surveys). However, European universities bring over one-third of inventions to market, with licensing the main channel. The Unitary Patent and UPC are expected to improve the European environment. Cross-country benchmarks (e.g. within the European TTO Circle) help identify best practices and areas for improvement.
