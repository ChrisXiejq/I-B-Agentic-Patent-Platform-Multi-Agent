# Patent Commercialisation Policy Frameworks

## Overview

This article outlines EU and national policy frameworks that shape patent commercialisation in Europe.

---

#### What EU policies support patent commercialisation?

EU policies include: **Horizon Europe** – R&D funding with exploitation obligations; **Unitary Patent** – simplified pan-European protection; **IP Helpdesk and Horizon IP Scan** – support services; **Innovation funding** – EIC, EIT; **Competition policy** – TTBER for licensing; **Single market** – free movement, harmonisation. The European Commission’s IP strategy aims to strengthen the IP system for innovation and growth. Policies are implemented in coordination with Member States.

**References:**

- [European Commission – Unitary Patent](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/industry/strategy/intellectual-property/patent-protection-eu/unitary-patent-system_en)

#### What are the exploitation obligations under Horizon Europe?

Beneficiaries must use their best efforts to exploit project results for four years after the project. If exploitation has not started within one year, they must use the Horizon Results Platform to find partners. Exploitation includes commercial use, further R&D, and standardisation. These obligations aim to maximise impact of EU-funded research. Non-compliance can affect payments and future eligibility.

#### How do national policies differ across Europe?

Member States have distinct: **innovation policies** – R&D tax credits, grants; **IP support** – national IP offices, awareness campaigns; **university policies** – ownership, revenue sharing, TTO support; **patent box** – tax incentives for IP income; **funding** – national research councils, innovation agencies. These differences affect commercialisation incentives and support. European coordination (e.g. through ERA) aims to reduce fragmentation.

#### What is the EU’s approach to SEP policy?

The EU balances incentives for innovation with access for implementers. The proposed SEP regulation would add: transparency (registry, essentiality checks); FRAND determination mechanisms; dispute resolution. The Commission has also addressed SEP licensing in competition cases. Policy evolution affects licensing strategies for SEP holders and implementers. Stakeholder dialogue continues.

#### How can stakeholders engage with patent commercialisation policy?

Stakeholders can: **consultations** – Commission and national consultations on IP and innovation; **associations** – ASTP, industry bodies, participate in policy dialogue; **research** – contribute to studies (e.g. EPO, Commission); **pilot programmes** – test new models. Policy shapes the commercialisation environment; engagement helps ensure policies are informed by practice.
