# Patent Strategy for SMEs

## Overview

This article outlines patent strategy considerations for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in the European market.

---

#### Why should SMEs consider patent protection for commercialisation?

Patents can: protect innovations from copying; attract investment and partners; support licensing and partnership negotiations; improve credibility with customers; and create leverage in disputes. For resource-constrained SMEs, strategic patenting—focusing on core technologies and key markets—can yield disproportionate benefits. The Unitary Patent reduces cost and complexity for European coverage, and EU support programmes (e.g. Horizon IP Scan) assist SMEs with IP strategy.

#### What patent filing strategies suit European SMEs?

Strategies include: **focused filing** – patents only for core, commercially relevant innovations; **geographic prioritisation** – key markets first (e.g. home country, major EU markets, Unitary Patent); **PCT/EPO route** – defer costs and keep options open; **trade secret vs patent** – balance for process or hard-to-reverse innovations; **defensive publishing** – where patenting is not justified. SMEs can use fee reductions (e.g. EPO small entity fees), national support schemes, and Horizon Europe funding.

#### How can SMEs use patents for financing in Europe?

IP-backed lending is emerging in Europe. UK banks (e.g. HSBC, NatWest) offer IP-backed products; research suggests IP-owning companies have lower default rates. In France, Sweden, and the Netherlands, patent pledging has been shown to increase debt capacity. SMEs should: document and protect key IP; obtain professional valuation when seeking finance; and approach banks with IP expertise. Many European SMEs are still unaware of IP financing options.

#### What commercialisation options do SMEs have for their patents?

Options include: **in-house implementation** – use patents in own products; **licensing** – grant rights to one or more licensees for royalties; **assignment** – sell patents for lump sum; **partnership** – joint development or co-marketing; **spin-off** – create new entity to exploit technology. SMEs in Horizon Europe can use the Horizon Results Platform to find partners. TTOs, innovation agencies, and EU IP Helpdesk provide guidance.

#### What EU support is available for SME patent commercialisation?

Support includes: **Horizon IP Scan** – free tailored IP and exploitation advice for SMEs in Horizon projects; **EU IP Helpdesk** – information, training, and helpline; **Horizon Results Platform** – matchmaking for project results; **Innovation funding** – EIC Accelerator, national schemes; **EPO and national offices** – fee reductions, information, and support. SMEs should engage early with these resources to integrate IP into business and innovation strategy.
