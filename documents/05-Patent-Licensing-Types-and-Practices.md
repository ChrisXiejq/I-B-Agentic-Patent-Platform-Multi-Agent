# Patent Licensing Types and Practices

## Overview

This article covers the main types of patent licences and common practices in European patent licensing.

---

#### What are the main types of patent licences in Europe?

Common types include: **exclusive licences** – only the licensee may exploit the patent; **sole licences** – licensor and licensee may exploit, no third parties; **non-exclusive licences** – multiple licensees allowed; **territorial licences** – limited to specific countries; **field-of-use licences** – limited to certain applications or sectors; **cross-licences** – mutual licensing between parties. The choice affects competition, revenue, and strategic flexibility.

#### What is the difference between exclusive and non-exclusive licensing?

An **exclusive licence** grants the licensee the sole right to exploit the patent in the agreed scope; the licensor cannot license others or usually exploit itself. A **non-exclusive licence** allows multiple licensees and the licensor to exploit simultaneously. Exclusive licences suit high-investment commercialisation; non-exclusive licences suit broad diffusion. In Horizon Europe, non-exclusive licences can generally be granted without EC authorisation; exclusive licences must not breach access-rights obligations.

#### What key terms should a patent licence agreement cover in Europe?

Important elements include: scope (claims, products, processes), territory, exclusivity, field of use, royalty structure (lump sum, running royalty, hybrid), sublicensing rights, improvement handling, warranty and indemnity, term and termination, confidentiality, and governing law. For FRAND-committed standard-essential patents, terms must comply with FRAND obligations. Licences may need to align with EU competition rules (e.g. Technology Transfer Block Exemption Regulation).

#### Should patent licences be recorded at the EPO?

Recording a licence in the European Patent Register (or national registers) is not mandatory but is recommended. It creates public notice of the licensee’s rights, can protect the licensee against later assignments, and may be required for enforcement in some jurisdictions. For Unitary Patents, licences can be recorded in the Unitary Patent Register maintained by the EPO.

#### How do patent pools work for licensing in Europe?

Patent pools aggregate patents from multiple owners into a single licensing programme. They offer one-stop licensing, lower transaction costs, and more transparent terms. The EU TTBER Guidelines treat well-designed pools as pro-competitive. Pools should use transparent creation processes, independent evaluators, safeguards against sensitive information exchange, and allow licensees to challenge validity or negotiate bilaterally. Pools are common for standard-essential patents (SEPs).
