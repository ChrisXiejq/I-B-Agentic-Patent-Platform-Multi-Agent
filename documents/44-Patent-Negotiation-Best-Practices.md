# Patent Negotiation Best Practices

## Overview

This article outlines best practices for negotiating patent licences and commercialisation deals in Europe.

---

#### What preparation is needed before patent licence negotiations?

Preparation includes: **IP due diligence** – ownership, validity, scope, encumbrances; **valuation** – estimate of value using income, market, or cost approach; **market research** – comparable licences, industry benchmarks; **FTO** – for licensee, third-party risks; **strategic goals** – what each party wants (revenue, access, exclusivity, etc.); **alternatives** – BATNA (best alternative to negotiated agreement); **team** – technical, legal, business. For complex deals, external advisors may be needed.

#### What are effective negotiation strategies for patent licences?

Strategies include: **interest-based negotiation** – focus on underlying needs, not just positions; **create value** – expand the pie (e.g. field/territory carve-outs, improvement handling); **objective criteria** – benchmarks, comparable licences; **relationship** – build trust for long-term deals; **documentation** – clear term sheets and agreements. For FRAND licences, good-faith negotiation is expected. EU competition rules (TTBER) constrain certain terms.

#### What common pitfalls should be avoided in patent negotiations?

Pitfalls include: **overemphasis on price** – ignoring scope, exclusivity, improvements; **unclear scope** – ambiguous claims, territory, field; **missing IP** – patents in family, future patents, know-how; **no validity protection** – licensee’s exposure if patent invalidated; **competition law** – hardcore restrictions, no-challenge clauses (void in EU); **governing law** – choice affects interpretation and enforcement. Legal and technical review before signing reduces risk.

#### How should royalty rates be negotiated?

Approaches: **benchmarks** – comparable licences, industry surveys; **valuation** – income method, profit split; **risk adjustment** – discount for early stage, uncertainty; **structure** – upfront vs royalty, tiers, minimums. Negotiation may start from licensor’s offer or licensee’s counter. Documentation of rationale supports compliance (e.g. transfer pricing) and future reference. For SEPs, FRAND rates are often disputed; courts and regulators provide guidance.

#### What role do term sheets play in patent negotiations?

Term sheets summarise key deal terms before full agreement. They typically cover: parties, IP, scope, territory, exclusivity, financial terms, term, key obligations. Term sheets can be binding (e.g. exclusivity period) or non-binding (save for specified provisions). They align parties and reduce later disputes. For complex deals, a well-drafted term sheet accelerates due diligence and drafting.
