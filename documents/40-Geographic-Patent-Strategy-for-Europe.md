# Geographic Patent Strategy for Europe

## Overview

This article discusses geographic patent filing and commercialisation strategy for the European market.

---

#### What filing options exist for European patent protection?

Options include: **national filing** – direct to each national office; **European patent (EP)** – single application at EPO, designating multiple states, validation in each; **Unitary Patent** – request unitary effect after EP grant for participating states; **PCT** – international application, then EP or national phase. Choice depends on: target markets, budget, timeline, and enforcement strategy. The Unitary Patent simplifies coverage for up to 25 EU states.

**References:**
- [EPO – Applying for a European patent](https://www.epo.org/applying/european.html)
- [EPO – Unitary Patent](https://www.epo.org/en/applying/european/unitary/unitary-patent)
- [EPO – PCT and European route](https://www.epo.org/applying/international-applications.html)

---

#### How does the Unitary Patent change geographic strategy?

The Unitary Patent offers one registration for all participating states (currently 18, expanding to ~25). Advantages: single renewal fee, no national validation, centralised UPC enforcement. Considerations: all-or-nothing coverage (cannot exclude individual states); opt-out available for traditional national litigation; renewal cost vs national validation. For pan-European commercialisation, Unitary Patent can be cost-effective and administratively simpler.

**References:**
- [EPO – Unitary Patent](https://www.epo.org/en/applying/european/unitary/unitary-patent)
- [European Commission – Unitary Patent system](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/industry/strategy/intellectual-property/patent-protection-eu/unitary-patent-system_en)

#### Which European countries should be prioritised for patent coverage?

Prioritisation depends on: **market size** – Germany, France, UK, Italy, Spain; **industry focus** – e.g. automotive (Germany), pharma (UK, Switzerland), tech (Netherlands, Ireland); **manufacturing** – where products are made; **enforcement** – jurisdictions with active patent litigation. The Unitary Patent covers major economies (DE, FR, IT, NL, etc.). Non-participating states (e.g. Spain, UK post-Brexit) require national validation if needed.

#### How does licensing territory affect geographic strategy?

Licence agreements define territory (e.g. worldwide, EU, specific countries). Geographic strategy should align with: licensee’s markets; exclusivity vs non-exclusivity; royalty structure (e.g. by region). Unitary Patent simplifies pan-EU licensing. For field or product restrictions, territory may be defined by geography and field. Cross-border licensing must consider EU competition rules (TTBER) and territorial restrictions.

#### What are the implications of Brexit for UK patent strategy?

The UK left the EU but remains in the European Patent Organisation. European patents (EP) can still designate the UK and be validated there. The Unitary Patent does not cover the UK. For UK-only or UK-focused commercialisation, national UK validation remains relevant. The UK IPO handles national applications and validations. Enforcement is through UK courts, not the UPC.
