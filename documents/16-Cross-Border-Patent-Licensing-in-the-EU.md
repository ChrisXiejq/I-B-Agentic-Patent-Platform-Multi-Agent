# Cross-Border Patent Licensing in the EU

## Overview

This article discusses considerations for cross-border patent licensing within the European Union.

---

#### What advantages does the EU single market offer for patent licensing?

The EU single market enables licensing across 27 Member States with harmonised rules on free movement of goods and competition. The Unitary Patent extends protection to up to 25 participating countries through one registration. The Unified Patent Court provides centralised litigation. EU competition rules (e.g. TTBER) provide a framework for licence agreements. These elements reduce complexity and cost for pan-European licensing strategies.

#### What competition law considerations apply to EU cross-border licences?

Licence agreements must comply with EU competition rules (Article 101 TFEU). The Technology Transfer Block Exemption Regulation (TTBER) provides a safe harbour for agreements that meet its conditions. Hardcore restrictions (e.g. price fixing, market allocation between competitors) are not exempted. Territorial restrictions may be acceptable in certain circumstances. Parties should assess whether their agreement falls within the TTBER or requires individual assessment.

#### How does the Unitary Patent affect cross-border licensing?

The Unitary Patent simplifies cross-border licensing by providing a single asset covering all participating states. A single licence can cover the entire Unitary Patent territory. No need to negotiate separate national validations. Enforcement is centralised through the UPC. This reduces transaction costs, simplifies royalty structures, and makes European licences more attractive for both licensors and licensees.

#### What tax implications arise in cross-border EU patent licensing?

Cross-border royalties may be subject to withholding tax in the licensee’s country, unless reduced or eliminated by double tax treaties or EU directives (e.g. Interest and Royalties Directive). Transfer pricing rules apply to related-party transactions. Patent box regimes in several EU countries offer preferential tax treatment for patent-derived income. Licensors and licensees should obtain tax advice to optimise structure and comply with reporting obligations.

#### What dispute resolution options exist for cross-border licensing disputes?

Options include: **national courts** – for national patents and non-UPC jurisdictions; **Unified Patent Court** – for European and Unitary Patents in participating states; **arbitration** – often preferred for confidential, cross-border disputes; **mediation** – for preserving relationships. Many licences specify governing law and dispute resolution (e.g. ICC, LCIA arbitration). The UPC’s central division and local/regional divisions handle infringement and validity for UPC patents.
