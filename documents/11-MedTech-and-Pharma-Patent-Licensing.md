# MedTech and Pharma Patent Licensing

## Overview

This article covers patent licensing practices and considerations in the European medical technology and pharmaceutical sectors.

---

#### What are typical structures for pharma patent licensing deals in Europe?

Pharma licensing often combines upfront payments, milestone payments, and royalties. For example, PhaseBio’s European licence to Alfasigma for bentracimab included a $20 million upfront payment, up to $225 million in milestones, and tiered royalties (low double digits to mid-twenties on net sales). The licensee (Alfasigma) handles commercialisation in the EU, EEA, UK, Russia, and selected Commonwealth countries. Such structures allocate risk and reward between licensor and licensee according to development stage and market potential.

#### How important is patent protection for MedTech commercialisation in Europe?

Strong patent portfolios are critical for attracting venture capital and corporate partners. The Dermis Pharma case illustrates that well-defined IP and qualified commercialisation expertise enable IP-assignment deals and funding. European patents covering key therapeutic areas, formulations, or delivery technologies can support regulatory strategy, exclusivity, and licensing value. Patent scope, remaining term, and geographic coverage directly affect deal terms.

#### What geographic considerations apply to European pharma licensing?

Licences may be structured by region (e.g. EU, EEA, UK) or country. Regulatory approval pathways differ (EMA for EU, MHRA for UK), and licensors sometimes retain responsibility for regulatory submissions while licensees handle local commercialisation. The Unitary Patent can simplify European coverage. Territorial carve-outs, exclusivity, and sublicensing rights need to be clearly defined in the agreement.

#### How do large MedTech companies approach IP licensing in Europe?

Companies such as Philips offer health technology IP licensing programmes that allow partners to access technologies, reduce development costs, and accelerate time-to-market. Licensing can be part of a broader strategy to expand technology adoption, generate revenue, and build ecosystems. Advisory firms (e.g. PharmaVentures) support European biotech and MedTech licensing with deal structuring, valuation, and negotiation.

#### What role do European patents play in drug delivery technology commercialisation?

European patents for drug delivery technologies (e.g. formulations, devices, routes of administration) can cover multiple indications and extend protection beyond the base compound. For example, BioNxt’s European patent for sublingual thin-film drug delivery covers up to 39 countries with protection until 2043, applicable to Multiple Sclerosis and other conditions. Such patents support licensing, partnership, and investment in European and global markets.
