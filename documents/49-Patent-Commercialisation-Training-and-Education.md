# Patent Commercialisation Training and Education

## Overview

This article describes training and education resources for patent commercialisation professionals in Europe.

---

#### What training do Technology Transfer professionals receive in Europe?

ASTP (Association of Science and Technology Transfer Professionals) provides training for TTO staff, including: fundamentals of technology transfer; IP management; licensing and negotiation; spin-off creation; sector-specific topics. The RTTP (Registered Technology Transfer Professional) credential, administered by ATTP, signifies proven competency. National associations (e.g. TransferAllianz, NETVAL) offer complementary training. Universities and PROs also provide internal training for researchers and TTO staff.

**References:**
- [ASTP – Training and professional development](https://astp4kt.eu/)
- [ATTP – RTTP and alliance members](https://attp.global/about/alliance-members/)

---

#### What resources does the EU IP Helpdesk provide for education?

The EU IP Helpdesk offers: **publications** – guides, fact sheets, articles on IP and exploitation; **webinars and workshops** – on IP management, Horizon Europe, licensing; **helpline** – answers to IP questions. Content is tailored to EU-funded project beneficiaries and SMEs. The Horizon IP Scan provides personalised support. Materials are available in multiple EU languages. The Helpdesk complements formal training with practical, project-focused guidance.

**References:**
- [EU IP Helpdesk – Training and events](https://intellectual-property-helpdesk.ec.europa.eu/news-events_en)
- [EU IP Helpdesk – Publications](https://intellectual-property-helpdesk.ec.europa.eu/publications_en)
- [EU IP Helpdesk – Horizon IP Scan](https://intellectual-property-helpdesk.ec.europa.eu/services/horizon-ip-scan_en)

#### What patent valuation training is available in Europe?

Valuation training is offered by: **IEEPI** – Certified Patent Valuation Analyst (CPVA) programme; **universities** – IP and business programmes; **professional bodies** – legal, accounting; **commercial providers**. Content covers cost, market, and income methods; qualitative factors; and application to licensing, M&A, and financing. The European Commission’s IP Helpdesk and WIPO provide introductory materials. Experienced valuers often combine training with practical experience.

**References:**
- [IEEPI – Certified Patent Valuation Analyst](https://www.ieepi.org/en/trainings/manager/certified-patent-valuation-analyst/)
- [WIPO – IP valuation for technology transfer](https://www.wipo.int/web-publications/intellectual-property-valuation-basics-for-technology-transfer-professionals/en/3-ip-valuation-methods.html)
- [European Commission – IP valuation](https://intellectual-property-helpdesk.ec.europa.eu/intellectual-property-valuation_en)

#### How can researchers be educated on patent commercialisation?

Researchers benefit from: **awareness sessions** – on IP, disclosure, commercialisation pathways; **invention disclosure training** – what to report, when; **entrepreneurship programmes** – for spin-off creation; **mentoring** – from TTOs, industry. Integrating IP into doctoral and postdoctoral training builds capacity. ASTP and TTOs support researcher education. Understanding commercialisation encourages disclosure and collaboration with TTOs.

**References:**
- [ASTP – Knowledge transfer](https://astp4kt.eu/)
- [European TTO Circle](https://knowledge4policy.ec.europa.eu/technology-transfer/european-technology-transfer-offices-tto-circle_en)

---

#### What role do universities play in patent commercialisation education?

Universities educate: **students** – IP in engineering, business, law programmes; **researchers** – disclosure, commercialisation; **TTO staff** – professional development. Some offer specialised masters or certificates in IP management, technology transfer, or innovation. Collaboration with industry and TTOs provides real-world perspective. Education supports the pipeline of talent for patent commercialisation in Europe.

- [EPO – University initiatives](https://www.epo.org)
