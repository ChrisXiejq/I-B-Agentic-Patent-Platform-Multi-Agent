# Patent Assignment vs Licensing

## Overview

This article compares patent assignment and licensing, and when each is appropriate for European patent commercialisation.

---

#### What is the difference between patent assignment and licensing?

**Assignment** is a permanent transfer of ownership. The assignor ceases to be the patent owner; the assignee becomes the new owner and acquires all rights (subject to any prior licences or encumbrances). **Licensing** grants permission to use the patent within agreed scope while the licensor retains ownership. The licensee obtains defined rights (e.g. manufacture, sell) for a period, territory, and field of use. Assignment is an exit; licensing is a revenue and diffusion strategy.

#### When is assignment typically chosen over licensing?

Assignment is common when: the patent owner exits the business or technology area; an acquirer wants full control for M&A; the seller prefers a one-time payment over royalty streams; the patent is a non-core asset; or spin-off or divestiture requires clean transfer. In Horizon Europe, assignments are allowed but the transferee must assume grant agreement obligations. Assignment may also trigger tax implications (e.g. capital gains) that differ from licensing.

#### When is licensing typically chosen over assignment?

Licensing is preferred when: the owner wants to retain ownership and earn recurring revenue; multiple licensees are sought; the owner uses the technology itself; flexibility to terminate or relicense is important; or tax or accounting treatment favours licensing. Licensing allows broader dissemination, shared risk, and continued control. Exclusive licensing can provide licensee incentives similar to ownership while the licensor retains reversion rights.

#### How are assignments recorded in Europe?

Assignments should be recorded in the relevant patent register to establish the new owner’s rights and protect against later conflicting transfers. For European patents, the EPO Patent Register can be updated; for national validations and Unitary Patents, the appropriate national or Unitary Patent Register is used. Recording requirements and procedures vary by jurisdiction. Failure to record may affect enforceability and priority in insolvency.

#### What considerations apply to partial assignments or licences with option to assign?

Some transactions combine elements: **partial assignment** – transfer of a share (e.g. 50%) with co-ownership; **licence with option to purchase** – licensee gains right to acquire the patent on agreed terms; **exclusive licence with right to sublicense** – licensee effectively controls exploitation. These hybrid structures can align incentives and address valuation uncertainty. They require careful drafting to define rights, obligations, and triggers.
