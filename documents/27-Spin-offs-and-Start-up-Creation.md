# Spin-offs and Start-up Creation

## Overview

This article covers patent-based spin-off and start-up creation in the European context.

---

#### How do universities use patents to create spin-offs in Europe?

Universities often create spin-offs by: transferring or exclusively licensing patents to a new company; researchers or TTO staff taking equity; securing seed investment; and providing incubation support. Patents protect the technology and attract investors. European universities have created thousands of spin-offs; the European TTO Circle reports 4,000+ startups across its member organisations. Success factors include strong technology, entrepreneurial talent, and supportive ecosystems.

#### What IP structures are common for university spin-offs?

Structures include: **assignment** – university transfers patents to spin-off (often for equity or deferred payment); **exclusive licence** – spin-off obtains exclusive rights, university retains ownership; **option to licence** – spin-off has time-limited option to negotiate licence. Equity participation by university and inventors is common. Agreement terms may include field or territory limits, sublicensing rights, improvement handling, and reversion if the spin-off fails.

#### What support do European spin-offs receive for patent commercialisation?

Support includes: **TTO assistance** – IP strategy, deal structuring, investor introductions; **incubators and accelerators** – mentoring, space, networks; **EU funding** – EIC Accelerator, Horizon Europe, national schemes; **IP Helpdesk** – Horizon IP Scan for project-based SMEs; **networks** – ASTP, EIT, national associations. Patent-focused support helps spin-offs protect, value, and leverage their IP in funding and partnership discussions.

#### How do patents affect spin-off valuation and investment?

Investors typically assess: strength and scope of patents; FTO; remaining term; geographic coverage; alignment with product roadmap. Strong IP can increase valuation and improve terms. The Unitary Patent can simplify European coverage for investors. IP due diligence is standard in venture processes. Spin-offs should document their IP strategy, maintain patents, and be prepared to explain commercialisation plans.

#### What challenges do European university spin-offs face?

Challenges include: **resource constraints** – limited funding and management capacity; **market validation** – bridging lab and market; **regulatory hurdles** – especially in health and cleantech; **geographic fragmentation** – scaling across European markets; **talent** – attracting commercial and technical expertise. Strong IP, TTO support, and ecosystem connections can help address these. The EU and Member States continue to strengthen spin-off support programmes.
