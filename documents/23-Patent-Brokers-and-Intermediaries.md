# Patent Brokers and Intermediaries

## Overview

This article describes the role of patent brokers, technology transfer intermediaries, and matchmaking services in European patent commercialisation.

---

#### What services do patent brokers and intermediaries provide in Europe?

Intermediaries offer: **matchmaking** – connecting patent owners with potential licensees or buyers; **valuation** – assessing patent value for transactions; **marketing** – preparing materials and presenting technologies; **negotiation** – structuring and negotiating deals; **due diligence** – supporting buyers and sellers; **auctions and sales** – running structured sales processes. They can accelerate deals and improve terms for parties with limited in-house capacity. Some specialise by sector (e.g. pharma, tech) or transaction type.

#### How do technology transfer offices act as intermediaries?

University and PRO TTOs act as intermediaries between researchers and industry. They: evaluate inventions; file and manage patents; identify and approach potential licensees; negotiate agreements; support spin-offs. ASTP and the European TTO Circle represent TTOs across Europe. TTOs often work with external brokers or consultants for specific transactions, especially high-value or cross-border deals.

#### What is the Horizon Results Platform and how does it support matchmaking?

The Horizon Results Platform is an EU service that allows Horizon Europe beneficiaries to publish project results and connect with companies, investors, and other organisations seeking technologies. It supports exploitation obligations when beneficiaries have not started exploitation within one year after the project. The platform facilitates matchmaking without requiring external brokers, though beneficiaries may also use brokers for complex transactions.

#### What fees do patent brokers typically charge?

Fee structures vary: **success fee** – percentage of transaction value (e.g. 5–15%); **retainer** – monthly or project-based fee; **hybrid** – retainer plus success fee. Some brokers work on success-only basis for proven technologies. Universities and PROs may use internal TTO resources or engage external brokers for specific deals. Fee arrangements should be documented in engagement letters.

#### When should patent owners use a broker versus in-house or TTO?

Use a broker when: no in-house licensing capacity; limited network in target market; complex or high-value transaction; need for specialised sector expertise; desire for competitive process (e.g. auction). Use in-house or TTO when: existing relationships; routine licensing; capacity and expertise available. Many organisations combine: TTO for day-to-day, broker for selected high-value or challenging deals.
